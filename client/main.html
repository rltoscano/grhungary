travel

airfare

Plane ticket prices and travel length from the U.S. west coast to Budapest, Hungary depend on the number of stopovers you have. For the summer season, air travel costs average around $1300 round trip and the duration of the flight around 16 hours. You can use any travel websites to book your flights. For example:

  • www.bing.com/travel
  • www.kayak.com
  • www.orbitz.com
  • and many others
weather

Summers in Hungary are very hot and dry and daily temperatures in July often reach above 86 Fahrenheit. Nighttime temperatures are cooler, though still warm. Sandals and shorts are comfortable attire both day and night.

directions to venue

If you plan to go straight to the location of the wedding from Ferenc Liszt International Airport, we will arrange transportation for you. If you arrive to Hungary a few days earlier and aim to visit Budapest, you can catch a bus or a train at the airport that will take you to the city centre. The best gift you could give us is to come to our wedding. However, if this is not feasible, you can choose to contribute to our honeymoon through this website. We do not have a gift registry, since, after living together for more than three years, we have everything we need (and some more) in our household.

We have chosen the beautiful Greek island, Kefalonia, as our honeymoon destination. Kefalonia is the most westerly island of Greece, lies in the Ionian Sea, in the channel leading into the Gulf of Patras. With its circa 780 square kilometres, the island is the largest of the Eptanissos (the Seven Islands). Turquoise seas, lush green vegetation, wild and sheer mountain scenery and a fascinating variegated geological profile have given Kefalonia a special atmosphere and beauty. Broad sandy beaches, small coves, and steep cliffs, steppe-like plains, fertile valleys, dense black forests of fir, a unique complex of caves and the highest mountain in the Ionian Islands, Mount. Aenos, standing at 1628 meters, are the most striking features of the island. Kefalonia is characterized by the creative forces of nature and three thousand years of cultural history.
